# Brand Voice — Edu Creativity Center
**Phase:** 1 — Content Source of Truth
**Created:** 2026-07-17
**Governing skill:** educore-public-website
**Applies to:** All public-facing content in English and Arabic

---

## 1. Voice Principles

The Edu Creativity Center voice is:

| Quality | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Professional** | Clear, organized, and trustworthy. No slang. No grammatical errors. |
| **Warm** | Friendly and encouraging without being casual. Speaks to the student as a capable person. |
| **Clear** | Short sentences. Active voice. Concrete verbs. No unnecessary words. |
| **Practical** | Describes real benefits. Answers real questions. Avoids vague abstractions. |
| **Encouraging** | Motivates without promising what cannot be delivered. |
| **Honest** | States only what is true and verifiable. Does not exaggerate. |
| **Student-focused** | Centers the student's needs, goals, and progress — not the academy's achievements. |
| **Easy to understand** | Accessible to non-native English speakers reading English content. Clear MSA for Arabic readers. |

---

## 2. Voice Qualities to Avoid

| Quality | Why to avoid | Example of what NOT to write |
|---|---|---|
| Arrogant | Alienates students and sounds untrustworthy | "We are the best English academy in Jordan." |
| Robotic | Creates distance | "Our pedagogical solutions facilitate measurable language acquisition outcomes." |
| Aggressively promotional | Feels manipulative | "Don't miss this AMAZING opportunity to transform your life FOREVER!" |
| Vague marketing | Provides no real information | "We offer innovative, holistic, world-class educational experiences." |
| Superiority claims | Cannot be substantiated | "No other academy matches our proven methodology." |
| Guaranteed results | Professionally and legally problematic | "Guaranteed fluency in 6 months." |
| Overly complex | Excludes students | Multi-clause, jargon-heavy sentences |

## 3. English Writing Rules

- Use clear, natural international English.
- Use active voice as the default.
- Keep sentences short (15–22 words average).
- Use accessible vocabulary — no need for a dictionary.
- Use consistent educational terms (see terminology glossary).
- Structure information: most important first.
- Use "you" to address the student directly.
- Headings: title case for H1 and H2; sentence case for H3 and below.
- Do not repeat the academy name unnecessarily within a page.
- Do not produce English that feels like a translation from Arabic.

### English — Approved Examples

**Weak:** "We provide innovative educational solutions that empower learners to achieve their linguistic potential."

**Better:** "We help you improve your English through structured lessons, real conversations, and feedback from experienced instructors."

---

**Weak:** "Our world-class curriculum is designed to unlock your creative potential."

**Better:** "Our courses are built around the skills you need: speaking confidently, writing clearly, and understanding English in real situations."

---

**Weak:** "Experience the transformative power of our immersive methodology."

**Better:** "Each lesson gives you practice with real language — not drills. You leave each session with something you can use immediately."

---

### English — Call to Action Style

- Direct and specific: tell the student exactly what happens next.
- Avoid generic labels.

| Rejected | Approved |
|---|---|
| "Click Here" | "Register for the Kids Program" |
| "Learn More" | "See the course details" |
| "Join Us" | "Apply for the Comprehensive Mastery course" |
| "Get Started" | "Take the placement test" |
| "Sign Up" | "Submit your application" |

---

### English — Heading Style

- H1: One per page. Describes the page topic clearly. Not a slogan.
- H2: Section topic. Uses keywords naturally. Not repetitive.
- H3: Subsection. Sentence case. Answers a specific question.

| Rejected | Approved |
|---|---|
| "Unlock Your English Potential" | "English Courses for Adults and Children" |
| "About Our Amazing Team" | "Our Instructors" |
| "World-Class English Education" | "About Edu Creativity Center" |

---

### English — Course Description Style

A course description must answer:
- Who is this course for?
- What will the student learn?
- How is it taught?

It must not:
- Promise fluency as a guaranteed outcome.
- Mention claims that cannot be verified (partner companies, awards, accreditations).
- Use competitor comparisons.

**Rejected:** "This revolutionary course uses our proprietary method to guarantee you reach C1 in just 6 months."

**Approved:** "The Comprehensive Mastery course is designed for learners who want to move from intermediate English to confident, professional-level communication. You will practise speaking, writing, grammar, and listening through structured lessons and guided conversation."

---

### English — FAQ Answer Style

- Answer the question directly in the first sentence.
- Add supporting detail in the following 1–3 sentences.
- Keep each answer under 80 words.

**Example:**

> **Who is the Kids English Adventure course for?**
> The Kids English Adventure course is designed for children aged NEEDS_CONFIRMATION. Lessons use games, stories, and activities that match how children learn naturally. No previous English experience is required.

## 4. Arabic Writing Rules

- Use clear Modern Standard Arabic (MSA — الفصحى المعاصرة).
- Keep sentences short and direct (not bureaucratic).
- Use the active voice when possible.
- Use familiar educational vocabulary (see terminology glossary).
- Use correct Arabic punctuation: Arabic comma (،), Arabic question mark (؟), Arabic full stop (.).
- Do not use dialect (عامية) in official page content unless the owner explicitly requests it.
- Write for RTL layout — do not assume any LTR structure.

### Arabic — Do Not Translate Mechanically

Each Arabic page must read as if it was written originally in Arabic.

**Rejected (mechanical translation):** "نحن نوفر حلولاً تعليمية مبتكرة تُمكّن المتعلمين من الوصول إلى إمكاناتهم اللغوية."

**Better (natural Arabic):** "نُساعدك على تحسين مستواك في اللغة الإنجليزية من خلال دروس منظَّمة، ومحادثات حقيقية، وتغذية راجعة من مدرّسين ذوي خبرة."

---

### Arabic — Terms to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| كورس | دورة |
| برنامج عالمي المستوى | برنامج متكامل |
| منهج ثوري | منهج فعّال |
| أفضل أكاديمية | مركز تعليمي متخصص |
| مضمون (as a guarantee) | مصمَّم لمساعدتك على |
| تجربة لا مثيل لها | تجربة تعليمية واضحة وعملية |

| Rejected | Approved |
|---|---|
| اضغط هنا | سجّل في برنامج الأطفال |
| انضم الآن | قدّم طلب الالتحاق |

### Arabic — Heading Style

- H1: واضح، يصف موضوع الصفحة مباشرةً.
- H2: موضوع القسم بصورة طبيعية.
- H3: جملة اسمية قصيرة أو سؤال مباشر.

| Rejected | Approved |
|---|---|
| أطلق إمكاناتك في اللغة الإنجليزية | دورات اللغة الإنجليزية للكبار والأطفال |
| فريقنا الرائع | مدرّسو المركز |

---

### Arabic — FAQ Answer Style

- أجب عن السؤال مباشرةً في الجملة الأولى.
- أضف التفاصيل الداعمة في جملة أو جملتين.
- لا تتجاوز 80 كلمة لكل إجابة.

## 5. Terms That Always Require Evidence Before Use

The following phrases may only appear on a page if there is a verified, approved fact to support them. They must never be added as generic marketing copy:

- world-class / عالمي المستوى
- best / الأفضل
- number one / الأول
- leading / رائد
- revolutionary / ثوري
- guaranteed / مضمون
- 100% success / نسبة نجاح 100%
- native speaker tutors / مدرّسون من الناطقين الأصليين
- [any number] graduates / خريج
- [any number] tutors / مدرّس
- AI-powered / بالذكاء الاصطناعي
- certified / معتمَد
- accredited / معترَف به
- partnered with [company] / بالشراكة مع
